1902.

As part of a group of 3 students, we were tasked to design, manufacture, and sell 15 barstools to pay for expenses and hopefully make a profit. Through the process of prototyping, we initally realized we were not fully utilizing the character and ability of the required material: 5/8” cold rolled steel. This idea of fully utilizing the materials we had became a driving decision making factor for the design of both the steel and the top seat material: reclaimed wood from a barn built 1902 in Benjamin, UT.

After many hours of trial and error with various materials and finishes, we were pretty happy withour final design. Using a secret concoction of floor finish, wood stain, resin (...and a few other things), we were able to successfully seal the reclaimed barn wood seat while maintaining its integrity. We were proud to announce we sold out of our stools at the sale. Not only did we break even, but we actually made a profit of $10.35.
